About me

“She is easy to talk to!”

I enjoy learning, working on myself through reflection exercises, community gatherings and conversations. I seek support when I sense that I need to be heard. I imagine creating enough spaciousness inside myself to have space to support a child, to nurture and care for one while also caring for myself. I am doing a small part of this now by caring for my grandmother.

I also make a conscious attempt to choose words that I use to express myself with, both when speaking and writing. I recently discovered through my practice of Yoga and by noticing changes in my body that “Life is dynamic and I have the power to contribute to it’s dynamism”. This loving reminder helps me feel empowered to make choices, and act on them.

I’ve trained in Psychology through my college years, and enjoyed it. My learning grew immensely once I got introduced to Non-violent communication. It showed me that I can look at a client as a fellow human being and bring my whole self to connect with them, instead of holding a position of an expert, which separates me from them. It freed me from the fear of being a mental health professional and being unable to express my needs to a fellow human; instead it showed me that I can openly express what’s alive in me as well.

Through my own awareness and practices, I’ve begun to understand and see how I can support others and add value to people’s lives by being present for them and helping them stay with their experience to see what emerges.

In my work so far, I’ve worked with schools and colleges, teaching Psychology, and as a Counsellor. I’ve also worked in groups and one-on-one with several children, parents and individual adults (and have been told “I am easy to talk to!” and that “I don’t arrive at a conclusion and give advice; instead I listen”).

I would love to work with you.
